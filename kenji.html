Play Time

The reindeer and koala are his favorite toys. Second tier favorites are the penguin, skunk, and purple thing.

He has a few long-chew toys that he sometimes settles down with; these have a rubber part and then parts on the end to gnaw on.

Muffin Tin Game

Grab a handful (12-15) of treats (I put them in my pocket). Where Kenji can't see or reach (like on a countertop), put 3-4 treats in random muffin tins and cover all 12 slots with tennis balls. Have Kenji sit before placing the muffin tin tray on the ground. Then point and say "find it!".

Once he's found them all, take the tray up and put another 3-4 treats in. Repeat until you are out of treats.

Routines

I try to encourage going outside via the laundry room door, but the den door and the kitchen sliding door are fine - especially if you think he might be about to pee in the house.

Sometimes there is no warning when he lifts a leg, but often he's sniffing slowly, sniffing furniture (especially a corner), and may weave back and forth on one spot. If you suspect he might be about to pee, loudly encourage him to go outside for potty. He'll usually turn his attention to you when you speak, but he likely won't go to the door - tug on his collar and help him find the door.

I generally don't pick up poop in the yard. If he poops somewhere people are likely to walk, I will. You are welcome to leave it and tell me when I get home. Poop bags are in the laundry room. Used poop bags can be tossed in the trash can outside the driveway fence gate.

Kenji now knows how to come back in the house via the doggie door. If you need him to stay outside, there is a panel you can slide in (on the exterior so he can see it). He will still try to scratch through it if he wants to go inside; that's fine - he'll eventually learn that the panel means the door is shut.

Leaving the House

The black RC pets martingale-style harness should be used if Kenji is leaving the house - either for a car ride or anywhere but the fenced part of the yard.

I use a leash splitter: one hook attached to his neck collar and one to his harness.

Beagles are notorious for slipping out of harnesses, hence the backup neck clip. The leash splitter should be set up so the neck collar side is usually hanging loose.

If you're taking him in your vehicle for a medical emergenyc, use your best judgement with regard to securing him during transport.

If you're walking him out of the house because of a household emergency (fire, flood, etc.), use a simple leash to his neck collar if time is of the essence. If you have a moment, find the harness/lease splitter along with the leash.
